Social Media and your email account

Details
Last Updated on Tuesday, 17 September 2024

The Department of Education is (in their words, very mealy:) "making an important change to help keep our students safe online, reduce the risk of cyber threats and make sure your student email accounts are used for their intended educational purposes only."

By which they mean, they are blocking emails from Facebook, Instagram, X/Twitter, Tiktok, Pinterest, Twitch, Discord etc.

These changes apply from 14 October - or Day 1 Term 4 - to your @education.nsw.gov.au email.

This doesn't affect your account's existence, it just means you could easily lose access to the account, for example, because you can no longer received login confirmation or password recover messages.

Why? "to ensure all student accounts are being used for their educational purposes, and to help protect you from risks online. Using your personal email address for these platforms instead of your student email address reduces the chances of someone gaining access to information in your school account."

Yes, this is a pretty lame threat analysis. But the sites themselves are blocked for you at school, so there is a reasonable level of logic in blocking email correspondence with the sites too. After all, the content you follow/are suggested on these sites is often not appropriate in the school context.

These changes do not currently apply to @student.sbhs.nsw.edu.au email addresses. But, generally, the school needs to defer to the Department's cybersecurity experts and harmonise its arrangements. So you should consider changing the associated email address for any Social accounts that use your school email address too.

Relatively small numbers of you are affected (because why would you use your school email address for this stuff? we can literally see all of it!). But we know also that some of you use Discord for para-school activity communication, and in that circumstance it may have made sense. Nonetheless, you should still change this out.

Perfect Attendance wasn't perfect

Details
Last Updated on Wednesday, 31 July 2024

Carlson Huang (07F) used the Feedback process to asked about something displayed in the Help section: Perfect Attendance.

It used to be that you could get award scheme points by having perfect attendance - literally zero absences. But then there was a pandemic and it become obvious that rewarding students for showing up to school no matter what (eg carrying a highly infectious disease) was not a supportable policy. So it was replaced by "Excellent Attendance": having an attendance rate of at least 95%.

Any absence, explained or unexplained, whole day or part day, reduces your overall attendance rate. Being out of class on school business, like excursion or sport representation or on a defined study day does not reduce your overall attendance. There's some 200 days in a school year; 95% is a very low bar to clear compared to the 100% of the past.

The relevant part of the Help section has been updated to reflect the current policy.

Adobe Creative Cloud Licence Changes - Updated

Details
Last Updated on Tuesday, 21 May 2024

The Department of Education and Adobe have been unable to come to an agreement to maintain the licence arrangements they had for you for Adobe Creative Cloud since 2009. Clearly DoE thought Adobe would blink because they've created quite a mess, including reneging on a promise to make licencing available to Year 7 students. The situation for you for Adobe licencing now is as follows:

  • If you study Visual Arts in Year 8 or above
  • If you study an elective taught by the Creative Arts or Industrial Arts faculty
  • If you have some other education-related need (eg you're on the Media Team)

Then you have had applied for and should have Adobe Creative Cloud licence. Otherwise, no soup for you. Yes, it's pretty sucky. Update: The Department are currently scrounging for licences, so getting additional licences assigned if you don't currently have one is virtually impossible.

Adobe Express - Update!
The Adobe and DoE preferred avenue for you to do your mild PDF, photo and video editing etc needs is to use Adobe Express. Adobe Express is an in-browser solution like Canva. You can access it with your school account at https://new.express.adobe.com/a/sbhs.nsw.edu.au.

If you have cloud content stored in your @education.nsw.gov.au account, you can use it with Adobe Express too.

Adobe Express is available from the Learning Resources part of the Global (Waffle) Menu available in the top-left of this portal and Canvas.

Just use Canva
Frankly most of what Adobe Express can do you'd be better off using Canva for. Canva aren't yet the evil empire, are based right here in Surry Hills and you can do a surprising amount with their products. All students have access to Canva for Education, the equivalent of Canva Pro. Just log in with your school email address at https://canva.com.

Canva is available from the Learning Resources part of the Global (Waffle) Menu available in the top-left of this portal and Canvas.

But I want to edit video
Firstly, both Canva and Adobe Express can make/edit video. Unless you know what you're doing in Adobe Premiere/After Effects, you don't want to use Adobe Premiere/After Effects.

Secondly, Adobe Premiere Rush should be installable as part of your Adobe Express access (but Adobe's documentation is poor on this matter, so double-check it is properly functional).

But if you have more complex needs, you should use DaVinci Resolve - all the power of the Adobe stack (up to 4K/60 4:4:4), free and built in Melbourne: https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/au/products/davinciresolve

Coming soon: Affinity Photo, Designer, Publisher
For a professional level alternative to Photoshop, Illustrator and InDesign, later this year Affinity Photo, Designer and Publisher will become available to you as part of their takeover by Canva and Canva's Education licencing. One in place, these plus DaVinci Resolve are basically a complete replacement for the creative elements of the Adobe Creative Cloud suite.

But I need to...
Given the above, probably you don't. However, note the third category above: if you have an educational need that requires you have the Adobe Creative Cloud suite, discuss it with your teacher and then they can discuss this with IT
